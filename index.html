PROBLEM STATEMENT

Traditional crab farming in the Philippines relies heavily on manual water quality monitoring, which is time-consuming, prone to human error, and often results in delayed responses to critical environmental changes. Farmers lack access to real-time data about water parameters such as temperature, pH levels, and dissolved oxygen‚Äîfactors that directly impact crab survival and growth rates. This leads to significant mortality rates, reduced yields, and substantial economic losses for local aquaculture communities.

PROPOSED SOLUTION

CrabTrack is an ESP32-based IoT monitoring system that provides real-time tracking of critical water quality parameters in crab ponds. The system integrates multiple sensors to continuously measure temperature, pH, turbidity, and salinity levels, transmitting data to Firebase for cloud storage and analysis. Farmers receive instant mobile notifications when parameters fall outside optimal ranges, enabling proactive intervention. An intuitive Android application provides dashboard visualization, historical trends, and threshold configuration.

SYSTEM IMPACT & CONTRIBUTION

CrabTrack empowers Filipino crab farmers with accessible, affordable technology to modernize their operations. By enabling early detection of adverse water conditions, the system helps reduce crab mortality rates and optimize growth conditions. The collected data contributes to building a knowledge base for sustainable aquaculture practices, while the cost-effective design ensures adoption feasibility for small-scale farmers. This solution bridges the gap between traditional farming methods and precision aquaculture.

AI MOLTING DETECTION

Advanced computer vision technology to predict molting cycles and optimize harvest timing

Our system uses computer vision and machine learning to detect when crabs are about to molt by analyzing behavioral patterns and physical changes. The AI monitors shell hardness, feeding activity, and movement patterns to predict molting stages with high accuracy, helping farmers maximize yield and reduce mortality during this critical phase.
